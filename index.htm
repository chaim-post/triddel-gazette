A letter from the commissioners of the fair

To The honored visitors of our great city of Triddel,

We, the commissioners of the fair, welcome you, the honored guests of our great city and especially to those who have come traveled hither to see the wonders of the first Triddel worlds fair. We are pleased to extend the most gracious hospitality of our city to you and to facilitate your enjoyment of all that this city has to offer have requested that the esteemed publisher of The Triddel Gazette produce a special edition of that great paper with the purpose of introducing you, our guests, to Triddel and to assist you in finding your way among the many sites that it has to offer. We hope that you find your stay comfortable and that you come to see Triddel as we see it, The Greatest City on Earth.

The Commissioners of the fair:
Sir George Townsfeed Walker
Mr. Francis Newberry
Mr. Lincoln Stanhope
Mr. Kuzma Kaminski
Sir Robert Brock

Triddel: An Introduction to the Great City

Welcome to Triddel. If this is your first visit you are likely overwhelmed by the immense splendor that is our city. Please allow me to assist you in your time of need by explaining some of the history and geography of this city. As well I will point out a few of the more important laws of our city for the benefit of those who come from less civilized lands.

A Brief History of the City

Triddel is an ancient city. It is so old that we have unable to determine who it's original inhabitants were or even what sort of creature them may have been. We do know that it was a center of planar trade for the Sehr-Ranee empire and that it was abandoned in the historic event that brought the downfall to that enduring nation. It was left vacant for untold years until Volhardend ships, blown off course by a storm, discovered it. They left a contingent of soldiers here and returned to their land. But the soldiers in exploring their new home inadvertently activated the great portal that powers the many magics that fill the city. This great surge of power alerted the Meritoire to the cities existence and a thus started the struggle for ownership of the city that would go on for the next thirty years. Control of the city transferred between the two nations several times throughout the end of the Aquitaine Conflicts six years ago, when the two countries, at the behest of the Elves established the new free city of Triddel.

Provided by professor Peire Brodeur

A Description of the City and it's Wards

The City of Triddel is built upon the surface of an immense cliff faced ring shaped island with a large plateau rising from the center of the interior cove. The interior cove surrounds a low circular wall of stone and in a few places overflows into a lake below. This lake flows out through a passage into a large cavern containing a gigantic whirlpool that is created by a large portal in the chamber's floor. Towards the south-west a large strait crosses cuts through the island, connecting the cove to the sea. This passage allows access to several wards of the city built into the caverns that open into the cove. In the north-west corner of the island a large building covered chunk of the ring's plateau has fallen down and settled along the edge of the island.

The city of Triddel has many wards with many diverse businesses, architectures, and residents. A few of the larger wards of the city are described briefly below. For more information please purchase Karl Murray's Guide to the City of Triddel where ever quality books are sold.

Two Score Towers is a large cluster of colossal towers located upon the island's central plateau. Two Score Towers is Triddel's center of magic and learning and is home to the illustrious Triddel University. Also located in Two Score Towers is The Reconditorium cognitione, the largest library in the world and home to some of the oldest tomes in existence.

The ancient portal district lies to the north-west of Two Score Towers along the island's ring. Here the scholars and scientists of the ministry of teleportation tirelessly work to restore the many portals that were the backbone of Triddel's commerce in days long past.

Sitting directly beneath the Portal District and reachable via numerous tunnels as well as a levitating platform is the crumbling buildings of Ruin's Wharf. Here you will find many fishing vessels docked as well as many of the cities less fortunate newcomers. While one is advised to be wary of the many dangers of the wharf it can also be a wonderful spot to search for relics of the past recently uncovered by one of the many digging operations of this fallen part of the ancient city.

Gear-home, the heart of Triddel industry, is located on a several raised platforms of rock jetting out of the island's inner lake. These small islands are covered in factories, refineries, and rail-lines. This ward manufactures everything from clocks to steam-ships and is a first stop for anyone wishing to purchase the latest in mechanical advances.

Built on the cliffs that border the strait are the wards of Port Bridge and Starboard Bridge. The Bridge wards are home to the Triddel observatory as well as the Sir Davy Raffles Public Zoological Preserve.

The newly constructed Triddel Worlds Fairgrounds, no doubt the purpose of your visit to our city, can be found on the western side of the island and is reachable by air-carriages from all wards of the city and from portals located in Two Score Towers, The Portal District, and Old wharf.

Some important laws of the city

Sir Robert Brock, Commisioner of Security for the Fair, has requested that we appraise those visiting Triddel of a few important laws and regulations of the city.

All visitors must present themselves to an officer of the guard before entering the city. Following an inspection and the payment of twenty copper pieces per person the visitors will be allowed access to the city.

All dangerous creatures, devices, and substances, including weapons, wild beasts, and explosive or corrosive chemicals, must be declared to the guard for inspection and an appropriate permit purchased by their owner before they are permitted in the city. After a permit has been purchased a mark will be affixed to the creature or device, not to be removed until such time as it leaves the city. Any such creatures, devices, or substances found within the limits of the city without a valid permit and mark will be seized by the guard and their owner penalized by either fine or imprisonment as befitting the nature of the infraction.

All citizens and visitors of Triddel are instructed to refrain from violence against their fellow creatures, and warned that any disturbances of the peace will be met with the swift response of the city guard.

Any who shall be caught stealing must return that which they stole and add a 5th to the theft's value. In addition the thief shall spend a single night in a cell with naught but a small amount of bread and water to contemplate their sin. If this shall be the third time the thief has been thus sentenced then he shall be exiled from the city. In the event that the thief is unable to repay their dept their possessions shall be sold, and if this sale shall not cover the dept then the thief shall be sold and their wages given to the victim of their theft until such time as their dept is repaid in full.
